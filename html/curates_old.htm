Block printing

Drop in to our block printing workshop led by textile designer Ren Valuzyte of Ren London.

Block printing is one of the oldest types of printmaking. During this workshop you will learn about this print technique and create your own linen tea towel. There will be an array of ink colours and pre-cut wooden shapes to choose from, allowing you to make your own unique designs.

This event is free to attend.

Saturday 30th September, Oxford shop (1 - 5 pm)
Thursday 12th October, Cheltenham shop (12 - 4 pm)

Boro patchworking

Drop in to our boro patchworking workshop led by London-based sewing school and fabric shop Ray Stitch.

In Japanese, there is a term called "mottainai" or "waste nothing". It is the core value of Boro (or Japanese indigo patchwork), the practice of mending or patching textiles. During this workshop you will learn simple sewing, piecing and patching techniques to repair a garment (please bring your own) or to create a textile inspired by Boro.

This event is free to attend. No sewing experience necessary.

Thursday 05th October, Notting Hill shop (12 - 4 pm)
Saturday 07th October, Bath shop (1 - 5 pm)
